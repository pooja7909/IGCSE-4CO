📚 The 6 topics

Topic order follows the official IGCSE Computer Science specification. Each topic is independent — study in any order — but Topic 1 is the gentlest start.

1 🧠
Problem solving
Topic 1 · Both papers · Algorithms & decomposition

Algorithms, flowcharts, pseudocode and trace tables. Standard algorithms — bubble sort, merge sort, linear search, binary search. Decomposition and abstraction. The foundation skill the whole qualification rests on.

The biggest topic — Paper 2 is built on it. Constructs (sequence, selection, iteration), data types & structures (arrays, records, strings), I/O and file handling, operators, validation, and subprograms with parameters. Full Python self-study app included.

Data
Topic 3 · Paper 1 · Binary, encoding, encryption

Binary, denary and hexadecimal — converting between all three. Signed integers (sign & magnitude, two's complement). Binary arithmetic and overflow. ASCII & Unicode. Bitmap images, sound sampling, RLE compression. Caesar, Vigenère, Pigpen, Rail Fence ciphers.

3.1 Binary 3.2 Representation 3.3 Storage 3.4 Encryption

4 💻
Computers
Topic 4 · Paper 1 · Hardware, software, logic

The input-process-output model and Von Neumann architecture. CPU components (CU, ALU, registers, clock, buses) and the fetch-decode-execute cycle. RAM, ROM, cache, virtual memory. Storage types (magnetic, optical, solid state). Truth tables & logic statements. OS & utility software. Compilers vs interpreters vs assemblers.

5 🌐
Communication & the internet
Topic 5 · Paper 1 · Networks, security, web

LAN/WAN/PAN, client-server vs peer-to-peer, wired vs wireless. Protocols (Ethernet, Wi-Fi, TCP/IP, HTTP, HTTPS, FTP, POP3, SMTP, IMAP). 4-layer TCP/IP model. Topologies (bus, ring, star, mesh). Cyber attacks (phishing, pharming, social engineering) and defences. Internet structure, DNS, IPv4 & IPv6.

🎯 How the exam works

Paper 1 — Principles of Computer Science

2 hours · 80 marks · 50% of grade
  • Written paper, externally marked
  • Multiple-choice, short open-response, open-response & extended open-response questions
  • Assesses all six topics at the principles level
  • All questions are mandatory

Paper 2 — Application of Computational Thinking

3 hours · 80 marks · 50% of grade
  • Practical on-screen examination, externally marked
  • Choice of language: Python, C# or Java*
  • Mix of multiple-choice, written and task-based questions
  • A pseudocode reference document is available during the assessment
  • No internet access permitted during the test

*This hub teaches Python only — by far the most popular choice and well-supported in past papers.
